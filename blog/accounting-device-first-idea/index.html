    Income counter wearable: first idea

    Citar
    Luarpy (2024-06-29). "Income counter wearable: first idea". Luarpy's blog. https://luarpy.github.io/blog/accounting-device-first-idea/
    TOC
    1. Idea
    2. Concept
    3. First desing

    Idea

    Some days ago, I found a short video on YouTube where a guy showed a gadget to get updates on the US public debt (I don't share any opinions on it). I found the idea of wearing a pocket-sized gadget at the workplace that would show a count of the money I earn to be attractive. As a rough idea, I thought of making a similarly shaped device that shows my income per month, day or hour as time goes by. This is something I have previously thought of a device to get a better idea of my productivity or income generated per hour worked.

    This device is designed to represent a person's income over a period of time, providing them with an expected idea of their income capabilities.

    Concept

    These are the purposes I expect from this device

    • Track/count my income for each hour, day, or month.
    • Portability: The shape and size must allow it to be handheld and fit into a pocket.
    • Informative and simple: There is no need for complex and multipurpose data on the display.

    First desing

    As a handheld device, it will sit on the desktop or be held in the hand. This way, it should provide an angled visual display that is comfortable to view and hold.

    The model will be 3D printed for prototyping and used in a development environment. For this purpose, it will need to be opened several times, so it makes sense to use fasteners with inserts into the plastic.

    The next steps will be to create a better design and a CAD model for a first impression.
